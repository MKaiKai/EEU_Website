    Frequently Asked Questions

    • Q: What insurances do you accept?
    • A: Unfortunately, we do not accept insurance. Insurance dictates the treatment as well as the time that patients receive. To give you the best possible plan for restoring your health, we take time to really get to know you and have a full understanding of your health history so we can arrive at the best plan for you to achieve optimal wellness. Therefore, we do not accept insurance or Medicaid/Medicare, so we can do what is truly best for you, not merely what insurance pays for. We do accept all major credit cards, debit cards, checks, cash, Zelle, HSA, and FSA!
    • Q: Will you be my primary care provider?
    • A: We are here to partner with you and your primary care provider, not replace them, and we will not prescribe any controlled drugs (such as opioids or benzodiazepines) or admit to a hospital.
    • Q: What is the policy on cancellation and missed appointments?
    • A: Your appointment time is reserved just for you. A late cancellation or missed visit is a missed opportunity for another client to experience our excellent services. As such, we require 24-hour notice for any cancellations or changes to your appointment. Patients who provide less than 24 hours’ notice, or miss their appointment, the deposit will be non-refundable.
    • Q: How is IV nutritional therapy beneficial to me?
    • A: IV nutritional therapy is, in many ways, more effective than taking oral supplements that contain the same nutrients. Instead of going through your digestive tract, which doesn’t result in 100% absorption, the nutrients go directly into your bloodstream for full absorption.
    • Q: What is your policy on weight loss program?
    • A: Weight loss medication is tightly controlled. We strongly recommend that you consult with your primary care physician regarding starting a weight loss clinic program and any health conditions and/or medications that you may be currently taking. 
    • Q: What is your prescription policy on weight loss medications?
    • A: Please be aware that lost, misplaced, or stolen prescriptions can NOT be replaced due to the controlled nature of the medications.
    • Q: Is there any alternative medication to stimulants?
    • A: If taking other medications that contradict with the appetite suppressants, or you simply do not want to take medication to assist with weight loss, we do offer an all-natural medication that is just as effective! The all-natural medications and supplements are through compounding pharmacy to be able to treat every patient according to their specific needs.
    • Q: Is telehealth available?
    • A: Yes. We understand that your time is valuable. We provide telehealth services for free consultations and medical follow-up appointments, if you prefer so. We just ask that you make sure you are in a well-lit space with solid WIFI connection, so you can get the most out of your visit.
    • Q: I am a person of color, so chemical peels are not for me?
    • A: Whether in Houston or Timbuktu, basically every one of us could use a chemical peel. Chemical peels are a natural treatment, where peeling agents encourage the skin to shed old cells. As long as you don't have any active skin infections or open sores, you're a great candidate for a chemical peel. At EEU, we only perform superficial peels and intermediate peels. We don't perform deep phenol peels due to their potential to cause pigmentation problems and scarring.
    • Q: Are chemical peels painful?
    • A: Superficial peels are not painful, as the peeling agents only penetrate the outermost layer of the skin ( epidermis). Afterward, your skin may feel as if you have a slight sunburn, but this passes in a day or so. Intermediate peels will involve some stinging and redness for a few days because the peel affects the entire epidermis and papillary dermis.
    • Q: Is a vitamin injection better for me than IV therapy?
    • A: Those who are short on time prefer injections, however, if you are feeling really depleted, we recommend IV vitamin therapy.
    • Q: What is a vitamin injection?
    • A: No matter how healthy you eat, you can still be lacking in nutrients. When you're feeling depleted, sick, or moody, a vitamin injection can help improve symptoms and aid in your immunity, well-being, and overall health.
    • Q: How does it work?
    • A: Using easy & quick injections, we can deliver key nutrients, vitamins, electrolytes, minerals and even medication into your body to improve your mood, wellbeing and health.
